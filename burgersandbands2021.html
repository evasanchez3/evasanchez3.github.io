About Me

I am a multimedia journalist with a focus on visual storytelling, my expertise is centered on videography and documentary. I am a graduate from the University of Maryland and received my B.A. from the Philip Merrill College of Journalism this past May. I have worked as a multimedia reporter for the last two years at The Diamondback, the independent student run newspaper at the University of Maryland. While also simultaneously interning as a feature reporter for College Park Here and Now and being a TA for the journalism schoolâ€™s video production class.

In the fall, I directed and edited my debut documentary, Make Space, which tells the story of Shaymar Higgs, a self starter who has been dedicating his time, money, and energy to his community for the last couple of years through his non-profit The SPACE: Free Art for All. This past spring the documentary screened at a local theater where all proceeds went to The SPACE. This project began because of a class I took, but what came out of it were the real world connections that I will have for the rest of my life, and the impact that this documentary had on The SPACE community. The work I did on this documentary has opened doors for Shaymar and The SPACE and that is truly what made me fall in love with documentary storytelling. My documentary was able to bring attention to a powerful story and that attention was able to spark change.
